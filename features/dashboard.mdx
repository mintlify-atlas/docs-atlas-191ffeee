The Dashboard (`/dashboard`) is the first screen you see after logging in. It gives you a real-time snapshot of your gamification progress and surfaces your most recent tasks so you can complete them without navigating away.

## What the dashboard shows

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Points" icon="star">
    Your total accumulated XP (`UserProfile.points`) displayed as a score card
  </Card>
  <Card title="Streak" icon="fire">
    Your `current_streak` in days, with your all-time `max_streak` shown underneath
  </Card>
  <Card title="Active challenge" icon="trophy">
    The title and start date of your currently active challenge, with a link to `/challenges`
  </Card>
  <Card title="Recent tasks" icon="check-square">
    Up to three of your tasks with inline complete buttons
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## User metrics

The dashboard fetches your profile via `userService.getProfile()` and displays the following fields from `UserProfile`:

```typescript
export interface UserProfile {
  id: string;
  name: string;
  email: string;
  profile_picture: string | null;
  points: number;
  current_streak: number;
  max_streak: number;
  created_at: string;
  updated_at: string;
}
```

<ResponseField name="points" type="number" required>
  Total XP accumulated across all task and challenge completions. Displayed as your score on the dashboard.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="current_streak" type="number" required>
  Number of consecutive days you have completed at least one task. Shown in the **Sequência** card.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="max_streak" type="number" required>
  Your all-time longest streak. Shown beneath `current_streak` as "Max: N".
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="name" type="string" required>
  Your display name. The dashboard greets you with the first word of your name.
</ResponseField>

## Real-time updates

The `DashboardPage` component calls `taskService.completeTask(taskId)` inline when you press **Concluir** on a task card. After a successful completion it silently re-fetches all three data sources in parallel:

```typescript
const [profileData, challengeData, tasksData] = await Promise.all([
  userService.getProfile(),
  challengeService.getActiveChallenge().catch(() => null),
  taskService.listMyTasks().catch(() => [] as TaskWithCount[]),
]);
```

Because the fetch is silent (no loading spinner), your score and streak update immediately after you tap **Concluir** without any visible flash.

<Info>
  The dashboard preview shows only the three most recent tasks (`tasksData.slice(0, 3)`). To see your full task list, click **VER TODAS** or navigate to `/tasks`.
</Info>

## Active challenge section

If you have an active challenge, the dashboard displays:

- The challenge title (from `challenge.template?.title`)
- The start date
- A **Ver Detalhes →** button that navigates to `/challenges`

If no challenge is active, a prompt appears inviting you to explore the challenge catalog.

## Navigating to the dashboard

The dashboard is available at the `/dashboard` route. It is the default landing page after a successful login. You can also reach it from the bottom navigation bar in the app.

## Data caching

The dashboard uses the `appCache` Zustand store to avoid unnecessary loading states on repeat visits. If cached data exists for `profile`, `activeUserChallenge`, or `dashboardTasks`, the page renders immediately from cache and re-fetches silently in the background.

<Tip>
  To force a fresh load, hard-refresh the page. The cache is held in memory and resets on each new session.
</Tip>
