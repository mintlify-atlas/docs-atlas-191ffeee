title: "Progress journal"
description: "Add daily notes to your active challenge to document what you did and how you felt."
---

The **progress journal** lets you add timestamped entries to your active challenge. Each entry records what you did on a given day so you can look back on your journey over the course of the challenge.

## How notes work

The `notes` field on a `UserChallenge` stores your journal as a JSON-serialized array of log entries. Each time you add a new note, the app prepends a new entry with a unique ID, the current timestamp, and your text, then saves the entire array back to the server.

```typescript
// The DailyLog structure used internally
interface DailyLog {
  id: string;       // UUID generated client-side
  date: string;     // ISO 8601 timestamp
  text: string;     // Your note (max 500 characters)
}
```

The journal is stored on `UserChallenge.notes` as:

```typescript
notes?: string | null;
```

Entries are displayed in reverse chronological order — the most recent entry appears at the top.

<Note>
  Each journal entry has a maximum length of 500 characters.
</Note>

## Add a journal entry

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Challenges page">
    Your active challenge card is displayed at the top of the page under **Desafio Ativo**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your entry">
    Type your note in the **Diário do Desafio** text area. Describe what you did, how you felt, or any obstacles you encountered.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the entry">
    Click **Registrar Progresso**. The entry is saved to the server and appears in the journal list immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To add or update notes programmatically:

```typescript
const updated = await challengeService.updateNotes(id, notes);
// Returns: UserChallenge
```

<ParamField body="id" type="number" required>
  The `id` of the `UserChallenge` to update.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="notes" type="string" required>
  The full serialized journal string. When adding a new entry, the app prepends the new `DailyLog` to the existing array and passes the serialized result.
</ParamField>

## Best practices

<Tip>
  Write your entry at the end of each day while the session is still fresh. Even a short sentence is more useful than nothing.
</Tip>

- **Be specific.** Note what you actually did, not just whether you succeeded. "Ran 5 km in the rain" is more useful than "Did it."
- **Record how you felt.** Motivation and energy levels give context to your progress over time.
- **Note obstacles.** If you nearly skipped the day, write down what almost stopped you. This helps you identify patterns.
- **Keep it honest.** The journal is for you. Accurate notes are more valuable than optimistic ones.

## Example entries

Below are realistic journal entries for a 30-day fitness challenge:

```text
Day 1
First session done. Went to the gym at 7 am before work. Only 40 minutes but I showed up. 
Feel nervous about keeping this up for 30 days.

Day 7
One week in. Missed the gym yesterday (late meeting) but made it up this morning. 
Learning that the morning slot is the only one that actually works for me.

Day 14
Halfway there. Sleep has improved noticeably. Still hard to get out of bed but 
once I'm there I'm fine. Increased weights on squats today.

Day 21
Three weeks done. The habit feels more automatic now. 
I stopped negotiating with myself about whether to go — I just go.

Day 30
Challenge complete. 30 days, 27 sessions (missed 3). 
Body feels stronger and the morning routine is locked in. Starting the next challenge next week.
```

<Info>
  Your journal entries are preserved even after you complete or abandon the challenge. You can review them in your challenge history.
</Info>
