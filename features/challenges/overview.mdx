Challenges are timed consistency tracks that you commit to and track over days or weeks. The system is built on two layers: a **template** that defines what the challenge is, and a **user challenge** that records your personal attempt at it.

## The two-layer model

Every challenge in Mais Hábito is made up of two separate objects:

| Layer | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Blueprint | `ChallengeTemplate` | Defines the title, description, and duration. Shared across all users. |
| Active instance | `UserChallenge` | Records your personal attempt: start date, status, notes, and completion. |

You create or browse **templates** in the catalog, then **accept** one to create your own `UserChallenge` instance. You can only have one active challenge at a time.

## Full lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create or browse templates">
    Open the challenge catalog to see existing templates, or create a new one by providing a title, description, and duration in days.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accept a challenge">
    Select a template from the catalog and accept it. This creates a `UserChallenge` with status `ACTIVE` and records your `start_date`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track progress">
    Use the progress journal to add daily notes. Your active challenge card shows how many days have elapsed out of the total duration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete or abandon">
    When you finish, mark the challenge as complete to earn XP and update your streak. If you need to stop early, you can abandon the challenge — this cannot be undone.
  </Step>
</Steps>
